# EMBRmon – Mining Battle Game Concept

EMBRmon is an optional NFT battle game built on Emberchain. It is completely separate from actual blockchain consensus—nothing in the game affects real mining performance or network security.

## NFT Minting

Players mint a unique EMBRmon NFT, each generated with its own creature design, elemental type (Fire, Electric, Ice, Nature, Shadow, etc.), stats, passive abilities, and a set of special attacks.

Minting is designed to reward early adopters while creating a long-term sink for the native token.

Example mint schedule:

* First 25 NFTs: Free
* Next 50 NFTs: 500 EMBR
* Next 75 NFTs: 1,000 EMBR
* Next 100 NFTs: 2,500 EMBR

(The exact pricing model is subject to change.)

Mint fees can initially be sent to a designated protocol wallet while the game's economy is being developed.

---

## Battles

Players challenge each other to turn-based battles.

Victory is determined by a combination of:

* Mining efficiency
* Strategy
* Luck

Mining efficiency provides each player with a starting **Battle Efficiency** score that functions similarly to HP in Pokémon.

Example:

Player A begins with:

Battle Efficiency: **175**

Player B begins with:

Battle Efficiency: **162**

Throughout the match, EMBRmon attacks reduce the opponent's Battle Efficiency.

Example:

Fire Blast:
-28

Lightning Strike:
-16

Critical Hit:
-40

Random cards, status effects, passive abilities, and elemental advantages all influence combat.

---

## Live Mining Influence

A player's actual mining efficiency continues to influence the battle while it is taking place.

If their mining performance improves during the match, Battle Efficiency may slowly regenerate.

However, regeneration is intentionally limited.

Example:

Initial Battle Efficiency:
175

Opponent attacks:
175 → 90

Mining performance improves.

Battle Efficiency might recover:

90 → 97 → 104 → 112

…but it can **never regenerate back to the original maximum through mining performance alone**.

Only certain healing abilities, cards, or game mechanics may restore HP beyond the regeneration cap.

This preserves the importance of combat while rewarding players who maintain efficient mining.

---

## Gameplay

Battles should feel like a mix of:

* Pokémon
* Trading card games
* Turn-based RPG combat
* Real mining performance

Each turn may allow players to:

* Use one of four creature abilities
* Play a randomly drawn battle card
* Activate an item
* Defend
* Heal
* Apply buffs or debuffs
* Trigger elemental combos

Luck plays a role through card draws, critical hits, status effects, and probability-based abilities, while strategy comes from move selection, timing, and resource management.

---

## Long-Term Vision

The goal is to create a game where mining becomes more engaging without affecting blockchain security.

Real mining determines part of your creature's strength, while gameplay determines the winner.

A player with a modest mining setup can still defeat a stronger miner through smart play, favorable card draws, elemental advantages, and good decision-making.

The ideal balance is approximately:

* 40% strategy
* 30% mining efficiency
* 30% luck

This creates a competitive game that rewards efficient miners while remaining fun and accessible for players with a wide range of hardware.
